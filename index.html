"One of the next evolutions in modern metal" - Everything Is Noise

Formed in late 2013 from an unlikely merging of jazz fusion and black metal projects, Asian Death Crustacean are a four-piece channelling extreme metal, jazz, post-rock and ambient/electronic music into extended compositions. They've spent a number of years refining their sound and playing shows around the UK, including supporting Three Trapped Tigers, The Physics House Band and Poly-Math. In 2019 they recorded and mixed their debut album, ‘Baikal’, with David Castillo and Jens Bogren at Fascination Street Studios, Sweden.

Baikal released June 2020 and combines the band's massive range of influences into an exploration of the theme of metamorphosis, each section or idea emerging as a development or response to the previous and combining into a seamless flow between an eclectic mix of genres.

“Hewn from cosmic post-rock, thunderous sludge metal and unsettling ambience”, Baikal is “a remorseless succession of hair-raising crescendos and fearless descents into near-silence” navigating “multiple atmospheres and tempos, from graceful and downbeat to furious and explosive.” - Dom Lawson (Prog Magazine)
